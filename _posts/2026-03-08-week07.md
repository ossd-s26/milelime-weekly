## How my group decided on a project

Deciding on a project was no easy feat. We had a "shortlist" of more than 15
projects on the board the day we were meant to decide. Luckily, we stratified
our candidate projects by several criteria (big, requires domain knowledge,
diverse contribution opportunities, single maintainer) and came up with several
projects to dive into individually in the following few days. Finally, after
emailing the creator of one of the one-man projects and not having received
a response, we decided to move forward with Oppia. Though I experienced initial
difficulties setting up its development environment, Oppia seems like a perfect
project to invest our time in. There are many ways to contribute, the system
on which it built is not overly complex, and the community seems open to new
contributors.

The development environment seems like an issue, but since I was able to
properly deal with it, I feel confident in our group's ability to do the same.
To work around issues that we may encounter, I plan to create a unified
developer environment for our group. I think that this could also be useful to
the Oppia contribution community as a whole and could even be a part of our
group's contribution.
